A Bangalore-based B2B SaaS company selling project management software ranked well on Google but was almost never named in ChatGPT or Perplexity answers. In five months, Digipuush took the brand from zero to 40+ tracked AI citations and a 62% lift in organic signup traffic by restructuring its content for machine extraction.

## What was the challenge?

The client had spent years building solid Google rankings for competitive terms like "project management tool for agencies." But when their prospects started researching with ChatGPT and Perplexity — asking questions like "what's the best project management software for small agencies in India?" — the brand was invisible. Competitors with weaker Google positions were being named and linked inside AI answers, while this client, despite ranking on page one, was skipped entirely.

The root cause was structural. Their comparison and feature pages buried key facts inside marketing copy, carousels, and JavaScript-rendered widgets that AI crawlers could not reliably parse. There was no schema markup, no answer-first content, and no FAQ sections mapped to the way buyers actually phrase questions to AI assistants.

## What did Digipuush do?

We ran a full AI visibility audit across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini for 30 priority buyer questions, then rebuilt the client's content around what these engines are trained to cite:

1. **Rewrote priority pages with answer-first openers** — every comparison and feature page now answers its core question in the first 40 to 60 words, in plain, extractable HTML.
2. **Added structured FAQ and comparison sections** mapped to the exact phrasing buyers use with AI tools, not just keyword-optimized headers.
3. **Implemented Organization, Service, and FAQPage schema** so AI crawlers could parse pricing, features, and positioning unambiguously.
4. **Published citation-worthy comparison content** — head-to-head pages and category guides written to be quoted verbatim with attribution.
5. **Set up monthly AI citation tracking** across ChatGPT, Perplexity, and Gemini to measure exactly which prompts surfaced the brand.

This mirrors our standard [AEO services](/services/aeo-services) process, paired with [GEO work](/services/geo-services) to shape how the brand was summarized in longer generative answers.

## What were the results?

Within five months, the brand moved from complete AI invisibility to being one of the most-cited options in its category across ChatGPT and Perplexity, with measurable downstream impact on signups.

| Metric | Before | After | Timeframe |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Tracked AI citations | 0 | 40+ | 5 months |
| Organic signup traffic | Baseline | +62% | 5 months |
| Priority pages with schema | 0 | 28 | 3 months |
| AI-surfaced buyer prompts | 2 of 30 | 21 of 30 | 5 months |

The 62% lift in organic signup traffic came from the combined effect of AI referrals and stronger, clearer organic pages. Because AEO and SEO share the same technical foundation, the restructuring that won AI citations also reinforced the client's existing Google rankings rather than putting them at risk.
