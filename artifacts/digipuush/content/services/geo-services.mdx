Generative Engine Optimization (GEO) makes sure ChatGPT, Gemini, Claude, and Perplexity describe your brand accurately — and mention it at all — when someone asks for a recommendation or comparison in your category. Digipuush builds the structured, fact-dense content these models are trained to trust and quote.

## Why do generative AI engines skip most business websites?

Generative models favor content that is unambiguous, well-labeled, and backed by structured data. Most business websites bury their core facts in marketing language, images, or JavaScript-rendered widgets that AI crawlers can't reliably parse. GEO fixes this by making your key facts — pricing, service scope, location, credentials — extractable in plain, crawlable HTML.

## What does Digipuush actually change on a website for GEO?

We rewrite key pages with explicit, fact-first paragraphs, add comparison tables and FAQ sections addressing the exact way people phrase questions to AI assistants, and implement Organization, Service, Person, and FAQPage schema so every fact on the page has a machine-readable counterpart. We also monitor how competitors are represented in the same AI outputs so we can close specific visibility gaps.

## How is GEO different from AEO?

In short: AEO gets you cited as the direct answer to a specific question, while GEO shapes how your brand is represented across every kind of generative output — summaries, comparisons, and recommendations, not just single answers. AEO is narrower and question-driven; GEO is broader and reputation-driven.

The two overlap heavily and are usually run together, but they optimize for different moments in an AI conversation.

| Factor | AEO | GEO |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Primary goal | Be the cited answer to a question | Shape how the brand is represented overall |
| Typical query | "What is the best CRM for Indian startups?" | "Compare the leading CRMs and recommend one" |
| Content focus | Answer-first pages, FAQs | Fact-dense pages, comparisons, entity clarity |
| Success signal | Verbatim citation with attribution | Accurate, favourable mentions and share of voice |
| Scope | Narrow, question-level | Broad, brand-level |

## How do generative engines choose what to cite?

Generative engines favour content that is authoritative, specific, and easy to attribute — and there is now research to prove it. The Princeton-led study that introduced the term GEO, presented at KDD 2024, tested which on-page changes actually increase a source's visibility in generative answers.

Its headline findings shape how we work:

| Optimization | Effect on generative visibility |
| --- | --- |
| Adding authoritative citations | Up to +40% |
| Including relevant statistics | Around +37% |
| Adding quotations from experts | Meaningful positive lift |
| Keyword stuffing | Around −10% |

The takeaway is clear: generative engines reward content that cites sources, includes data, and reads credibly — and they penalise the keyword-stuffing tactics that older SEO relied on.
