## What drives the cost of AEO services?

AEO pricing scales with scope rather than being a single flat rate. The main factors are how many pages need restructuring, how much new content is produced each month, whether technical SEO and link building are included, and how deep the monthly reporting goes. A small local business with ten key pages needs far less work than a large e-commerce brand with thousands of product pages, and pricing reflects that.

## AEO vs SEO pricing: how do they compare?

A common question is whether AEO costs more or less than traditional SEO. The honest answer is that they price differently because they do different work. SEO retainers often carry a large ongoing cost for link building and technical work at scale, while AEO concentrates spend on content structure, schema, and citation tracking. In practice, a standalone AEO engagement can sit at the lower end of a comparable SEO budget — but most brands do not buy them separately.

Because AEO and SEO share the same technical foundation, bundling them is usually more cost-effective than paying two providers. ## What hidden costs should you watch for?

When comparing quotes, look past the headline monthly fee for costs that can quietly inflate the real price:

1. **Setup or onboarding fees** — reasonable for large sites, but they should be disclosed upfront.
2. **Content caps** — some retainers include only a fixed number of pages per month, with expensive overages.
3. **Reporting add-ons** — citation tracking should be included, not billed separately.
4. **Long lock-in contracts** — a confident agency does not need to trap you for a year.
5. **Per-engine charges** — monitoring across ChatGPT, Perplexity, Gemini, and AI Overviews should be standard, not tiered.

A transparent provider folds these into a clear monthly price. If a quote looks cheap but hides caps and add-ons, the true cost can be far higher than a straightforward all-inclusive retainer.

## How does AEO pricing scale as you grow?

AEO spend should track the size of your opportunity, not just your company. Early on, a focused retainer that restructures your ten to twenty most valuable pages and builds citations for your core buyer questions is usually enough. As you expand into new services, categories, or locations, the number of questions worth owning grows, and the scope — and cost — grows with it.

The healthy pattern is to start at a level you can sustain, prove the return through monthly citation and conversion reporting, and then reinvest as the results justify it. Because AEO gains compound — each cited page strengthens your overall authority — scaling up tends to get more efficient over time, not less.
